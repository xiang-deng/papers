AI-powered programming language generation (PLG) models have gained increasing attention due to their ability to generate source code of programs in a few seconds with a plain program description. Despite their remarkable performance, many concerns are raised over the potential risks of their development and deployment, such as legal issues of copyright infringement induced by training usage of licensed code, and malicious consequences due to the unregulated use of Cites: Foundation Models and Fair Use
