The MPAA rating provides a guide for parents to decide if a movie is suitable for their children, and determines who is allowed into movie screenings. If the assigned rating does not match with that intended by the movie makers, the movie has to go through extra changes. Predicting this rating from the movie scripts would allow for the changes to be done even before the shooting starts, when they are the cheapest.
