Theoretical linguistics is focussed on constructing theories that unify a variety of phenomena. A key characteristics of such theories is compositionality. The core of this property is the requirement that the content of a phrase is a function of the contents of its constituents. A weakness of many such theories is that they are brittle. That is, they break easily when applied at scale, because they do not cover the range of variation exhibited in real corpora.
