Compiling comprehensive repositories of commonsense knowledge is a long-standing problem in AI. Many concerns revolve around the issue of reporting bias, ie, that frequency in text sources is not a good proxy for relevance or truth. This paper explores whether children s texts hold the key to commonsense knowledge compilation, based on the hypothesis that such content makes fewer assumptions on the reader s knowledge, and therefore spells out commonsense more explicitly.
