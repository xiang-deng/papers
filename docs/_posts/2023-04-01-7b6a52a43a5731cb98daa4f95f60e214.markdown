Public opinion reflects and shapes societal behavior, but the traditional survey-based tools to measure it are limited. We introduce a novel approach to probe media diet models--language models adapted to online news, TV broadcast, or radio show content--that can emulate the opinions of subpopulations that have consumed a set of media.
