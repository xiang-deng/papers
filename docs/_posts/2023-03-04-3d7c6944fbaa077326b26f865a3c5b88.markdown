As the use of interactive AI systems becomes increasingly prevalent in our daily lives, it is crucial to understand how individuals feel when interacting with such systems. In this work, we investigate the comfort level of individuals when interacting with intent-predicting AI systems and identify the factors of influence. We introduce a study protocol to analyze human comfortability when interacting with intent-predicting AI systems and execute the study with over a dozen participants.
