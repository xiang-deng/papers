This paper investigates whether and how occupational segregation affects the digital economy. Despite the continuous growth of entrepreneurial activity in the digital, little is known about the demographic characteristics of people actively engaging with it and benefiting from it. Further, while popular discourse portrays the digital as a level playing field for economic engagement, the literature has yet to empirically test these claims.
