Human language richly invokes our intuitive physical knowledge. We talk about physical objects, scenes, properties, and events; and we can make predictions and draw inferences about physical worlds described entirely in language. Understanding this everyday language requires inherently probabilistic reasoningover possible physical worlds invoked in language and over uncertainty inherent to those physical worlds.
