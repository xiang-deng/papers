Data-driven tools are increasingly used to make consequential decisions. They have begun to advise employers on which job applicants to interview, judges on which defendants to grant bail, lenders on which homeowners to give loans, and more. In such settings, different data-driven rules result in different decisions. The problem is: to every data-driven rule, there are exceptions.
