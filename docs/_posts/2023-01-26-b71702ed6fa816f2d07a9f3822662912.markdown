Seven years ago, BLS staff read and manually classified hundreds of thousands of written descriptions of work‐related injury and illness each year. Today, most classifications are performed automatically by machine‐learning models, which evaluations suggest are more accurate than trained humans. This chapter discusses how BLS addressed some of the many challenges inherent in this transition.
