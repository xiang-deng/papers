Natural language processing researchers develop models of grammar, meaning and human communication based on written text. Due to task and data differences, what is considered text can vary substantially across studies. A conceptual framework for systematically capturing these differences is lacking. We argue that clarity on the notion of text is crucial for reproducible and generalizable NLP. Towards that goal, we propose common terminology to discuss the production and transformation of Cites: FRUIT: Faithfully Reflecting Updated Information in Text
