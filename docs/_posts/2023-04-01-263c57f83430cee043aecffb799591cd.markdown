Plural counterfactual examples have been proposed to explain the prediction of a classifier by offering a user several instances of minimal modifications that may be performed to change the prediction. Yet, such explanations may provide too much information, generating potential confusion for the end-users with no specific knowledge, neither on the machine learning, nor on the application domains.
