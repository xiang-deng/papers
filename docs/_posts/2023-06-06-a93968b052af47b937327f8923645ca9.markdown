Abstract Knowledge-grounded dialogue is a task that utilizes external knowledge to generate appropriate and fluent responses to statements. Owing to the relevance of generating responses based on relevant knowledge in diverse fields, the knowledge selection task has been spotlighted. In this study, we propose a novel selection model that applies contrastive-learning with negative sampling loss to create dialogue-centric representation of knowledge.
