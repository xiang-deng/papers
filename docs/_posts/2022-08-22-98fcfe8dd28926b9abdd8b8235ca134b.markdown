Stylistic variations in text primarily manifest themselves at different levels of textual features: lexical features (eg, word choice), syntactic features (eg, preference for the passive voice) and even pragmatics, while preserving the original meaning of the given text (DiMarco and Hirst, 1990). Style can vary depending on the environment and target audience.
