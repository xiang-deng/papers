Knowledge about outcomes is critical for complex event understanding but is hard to acquire. We show that by pre-identifying a participant in a complex event, crowd workers are able to (1) infer the collective impact of salient events that make up the situation,(2) annotate the volitional engagement of participants in causing the situation, and (3) ground the outcome of the situation in state changes of the participants.
