Traditional theories of grammar posit that sentence formation begins with the underlying hierarchical structure generated by the syntactic component, which is then given meaning and sound interpretations by the semantic and phonological components (Chomsky, 1965). This dissertation provides evidence that word ordering is conditioned by the avoidance of phonologically-marked structures, challenging strictly feed-forward theories of grammar.
