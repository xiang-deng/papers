Unexpected responses or repeated clarification questions from conversational agents detract from the users experience with technology meant to streamline their daily tasks. To reduce these frictions, Query Rewriting (QR) techniques replace transcripts of faulty queries with alternatives that lead to responses that satisfy the users needs. Despite their successes, existing QR approaches are limited in their ability to fix queries that require considering users personal preferences.
