There is a widely held view that visual representations (images) do not depict negation, for example, as expressed by the sentence,the train is not coming. The present study focuses on the realworld visual representations of photographs and comic (manga) illustrations and empirically challenges the question of whether humans and machines, that is, modern deep neural networks, can recognize visual representations as expressing negation.
