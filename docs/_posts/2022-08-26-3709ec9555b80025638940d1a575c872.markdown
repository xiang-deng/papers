Determining the index of Simon s congruence is a long outstanding open problem. Two words u and v are called Simon congruent if they have the same set of scattered factors (also known as subwords or subsequences), which are parts of the word in the correct order but not necessarily consecutive, eg, oath is a scattered factor of logarithm but tail is not.
