Dialog systems are often designed or trained to output human-like responses. However, some responses may be impossible for a machine to truthfully say (eg that movie made me cry ). Highly anthropomorphic responses might make users uncomfortable or implicitly deceive them into thinking they are interacting with a human. We collect human ratings on the feasibility of approximately 900 two-turn dialogs sampled from 9 diverse data sources.
