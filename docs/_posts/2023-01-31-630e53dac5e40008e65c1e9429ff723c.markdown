Learning to reject is a special kind of self-awareness (the ability to know what you do not know), which is an essential factor for humans to become smarter. Although machine intelligence has become very accurate nowadays, it lacks such kind of self-awareness and usually acts as omniscient, resulting in overconfident errors. This article presents a comprehensive overview of this topic from three perspectives: confidence, calibration, and discrimination.
