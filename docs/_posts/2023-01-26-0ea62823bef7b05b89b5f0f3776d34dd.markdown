Linguistic typology is generally characterized by strong data reduction, stemming from the use of binary or categorical classifications. An example are the categories commonly used in describing word order: adjective-noun vs nounadjective; genitive-noun vs noun-genitive; etc. Token-based typology is part of an answer towards more fine-grained and appropriate measurement in typology.
