Semantic Heterogeneity is conventionally understood as the existence of variance in the representation of a target reality when modelled, by independent parties, in different databases, schemas and/or data. We argue that the mere encoding of variance, while being necessary, is not sufficient enough to deal with the problem of representational heterogeneity, given that it is also necessary to encode the unifying basis on which such variance is manifested.
