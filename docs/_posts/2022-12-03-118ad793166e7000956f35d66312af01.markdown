Topic models are a statistical model for discovering so-called  topics  as they occur in large collections of texts. They are an unsupervised technique to cluster documents based on the co-occurrence of words. That is, the input for a topic model consists of a matrix that lists the frequency of features (typically: words) in each document–a so-called  document-feature matrix .
