When reading a story, humans can rapidly understand new fictional characters with a few observations, mainly by drawing analogy to fictional and real people they met before in their lives. This reflects the few-shot and meta-learning essence of humans inference of characters mental states, ie, humans theory-of-mind (ToM), which is largely ignored in existing research.
