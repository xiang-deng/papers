Today s robots often assume that their behavior should be transparent. These transparent (eg, legible, explainable) robots intentionally choose actions that convey their internal state to nearby humans. But while transparent behavior seems beneficial, is it actually optimal? In this paper we consider collaborative settings where the human and robot have the same objective, and the human is uncertain about the robot s type (ie, the robot s internal state).
